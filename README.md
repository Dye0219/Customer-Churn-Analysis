🎯Objectives:

• Performed data cleaning and data consistency using PostgreSQL by handling missing values, standardize categorical fields, and validating calculations across tenure, monthly, and total charges.

• Analyzed customer demographics, service features, contracts, and billing attributes to identify key factors contributing to customer churn.

• Developed SQL-based churn indicators and customer segments to support targeted retention strategies and reduce overall churn.

📊Key Insights:

• The overall customer churn rate is 26.54%, indicating a significant retention challenge.

• Senior citizens are more likely to churn than not senior citizen.

• Customers using Fiber Optic internet service experience the highest churn rate compared to DSL.

• Customers who subscribe to multiple services along with Tech Support, Online Security, Online Backup, or Device Protection are less prone to churn.

• Customers with less than one year of tenure are the most likely to churn, while customers who reach one year or more of tenure show improved retention.

• Month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate. Although they carry the highest average monthly charges, they generate lower revenue due to shorter customer lifespans.

• Electronic Check is the payment method with the highest churn rate. These customers have higher average monthly charges but contribute less revenue. Additionally, this payment method is associated with higher churn among customers acquired paperless billing.

• Customer churn has resulted in an estimated revenue loss of approximately 17.83%.
